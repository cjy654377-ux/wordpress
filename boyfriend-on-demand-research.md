# Boyfriend on Demand - Complete Research Notes

## 1. BASIC INFO
- **Korean Title**: 월간남친 (Monthly Boyfriend)
- **English Title**: Boyfriend on Demand
- **Genre**: Comedy, Drama, Fantasy, Romance

- **Runtime**: ~55-60 minutes per episode
- **Release Date**: March 6, 2026
- **Platform**: Netflix Original (global simultaneous release)

## 4. PLOT SUMMARY (Spoiler-Free)
Seo Mi-rae is an exhausted webtoon producer who has given up on real-world romance. She subscribes to a virtual reality dating service that offers 901 virtual boyfriends -- each one programmed to be an ideal match. From wealthy heirs to secret agents, she can experience any romantic fantasy she desires. But as Mi-rae becomes increasingly immersed in the simulation, the boundaries between her digital romances and real life begin to blur -- especially when her real-world colleague Park Kyeong-nam enters the picture.

## 7. JISOO ACTING CONTROVERSY

### Pre-Release Debate
- Korea Times ran article: "Can Jisoo finally silence 'bad acting' critics?"
- Acting stigma followed her since Snowdrop debut

### Post-Release Criticism
- Korean critics highlighted: muffled/nasal vocal delivery, struggles with emotional scenes, diction issues
- Described as "stagnant" and "underwhelming" by Korean media
- Physical comedy moments feel "less natural"
- Vocalization called her "chronic limitation"

### Fan Defense
- International fans argued backlash feels "forced"
- Sportskeeda: "Viewers defend BLACKPINK's Jisoo against criticism over her 'stiff' and 'awkward' acting"
- Starnews: "Abroad, they're complimenting me" -- reevaluation angle

### Additional Controversy
- Jisoo received backlash for saying she **didn't cry at the final filming session** while everyone else (including directors) was crying
- Netizens questioned her passion/dedication to acting
- KBizoom headline: "Jisoo Doesn't Even Have Talent, So Why Doesn't She at Least Try to Work Harder?"

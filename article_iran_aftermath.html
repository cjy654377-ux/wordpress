1. Refugee Crisis — Potentially the Largest in History

Iran has a population of 88 million people. UNHCR estimates suggest that even if just 10% are displaced, it would rival the largest refugee movements this century. If 25% flee, it would increase the global refugee population by 75%.

Critical Details:
  • Turkey views a mass migration wave as an existential threat to social stability — already battling 31% inflation
  • Iran previously hosted 750,000 registered and 2.6 million undocumented Afghan refugees — now themselves displaced
  • The December 2025–January 2026 Iranian protests had already killed an estimated 5,000–36,500 people before the war
  • Child malnutrition is spiking in strike zones as food supply chains, water, and storage infrastructure are destroyed

3. Global Supply Chain Meltdown Beyond Oil

The Strait of Hormuz isn't just about oil. It's the world's most critical maritime chokepoint, and its disruption is sending shockwaves through industries far beyond energy.

Supply Chain Damage Report:
  • Hormuz tanker traffic down 70% — over 150 ships anchored outside the strait
  • 33% of global fertilizer trade (sulphur, ammonia) passes through Hormuz — now at risk
  • Petrochemical feedstock costs up 15–25% — affecting plastics, adhesives, and specialty chemicals
  • Automotive industry faces soaring energy costs and disrupted EV/ICE supply chains
  • Houthis resumed Red Sea attacks — creating unprecedented dual-chokepoint crisis
  • India's Chabahar Port (strategic investment in Iran) severely disrupted

With Houthis now attacking shipping in the Red Sea again after 3.5 months of calm, two of the world's three most critical maritime chokepoints are simultaneously active conflict zones — an unprecedented scenario in modern trade history.

7. Aviation Paralysis — The Skies Go Silent

Aviation Impact:

  • 10 countries closed or severely restricted airspace: Iran, Iraq, Israel, Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Syria, Jordan, UAE

  • QatarEnergy halted LNG production after attacks on its facilities

11. Nuclear Proliferation — The Domino Effect

This may be the most consequential long-term risk of all. Despite the strikes targeting nuclear facilities, analysts warn of "the limits of force against Iran's atomic program."

The Nuclear Calculus:
  • Iran still possesses 440.9 kg of uranium enriched to 60% purity — a short technical step from weapons-grade 90%
  • Iran retains the scientific expertise and infrastructure to revive its nuclear fuel cycle
  • Saudi Crown Prince MBS: "If Iran gets one, we have to get one"
  • IAEA chief Grossi warned an Iranian weapon could trigger broad Middle East nuclear proliferation
  • UAE, Turkey, and Egypt are all watching — a potential 4-5 nation nuclear cascade
"Iran is the most likely country to exit the NPT or develop nuclear weapons in violation of treaty obligations in 2026. The parties' views on enrichment, the missile program, and regional policy remain fundamentally incompatible." — Arms Control Association

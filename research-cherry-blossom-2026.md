**2026 is 2-7 days EARLIER than average.** Warmer-than-usual spring temperatures are pushing blooms ahead of historical norms across all regions.

## 3. Major Cherry Blossom Festivals 2026

### A) Jinhae Gunhangje Festival (진해군항제)
- **Dates:** March 27 – April 5, 2026 (10 days)
- **Edition:** 64th annual
- **Opening Ceremony:** March 27
- **Night Festa (Gunhang Night Festa):** March 30 – April 5, 19:00-21:00 at Jungwon Rotary
- **Admission:** FREE (all events free)
- **Scale:** 350,000 cherry trees; 4+ million visitors over 10 days
- **Events:** K-POP dance competitions, naval honor guard performances, air force aerobatic flights, maritime fireworks, military uniform experiences, weapon system exhibitions, Naval Academy public tours
- **No drones allowed** (military-sensitive area)

**How to Get There:**
- **From Busan (recommended):** Busan Seobu Intercity Bus Terminal (Sasang Station, Metro Line 2) → Jinhae. Fare: 6,100 KRW one-way, ~45-60 min, buses every 30-40 min (27 daily round trips, 6AM-10PM). No assigned seating.
- **From Seoul by bus:** Dong Seoul Terminal (3 daily) or Seoul Nambu Terminal (4 daily). Fare: 35,700-46,900 KRW, 4.5+ hours.
- **From Seoul by KTX:** KTX to Changwon/Masan Station + local bus. ~50,000 KRW total, 2.5 hours.
- **Day tour packages:** 40,000-60,000 KRW from Seoul (via Klook, KKday, KoreaTravelEasy)
- **Shuttle buses during festival:** 4 free color-coded shuttle buses on weekends (blue, yellow, red, purple). Green loop bus on weekdays.
- **No coin lockers** at Jinhae Bus Terminal. Store luggage in Busan first.

**Best Strategy:** Stay in Busan or Changwon, day-trip to Jinhae. Local accommodation is limited and overpriced.

**Must-Visit Spots in Jinhae:**
1. **Yeojwacheon Stream (여좌천)** — 1.2 km walk from terminal. Most iconic spot. Go to back sections beyond the crowds for better photos. Best at sunrise or early morning.
2. **Gyeonghwa Station (경화역)** — Vintage train car surrounded by cherry blossom tunnel. Instagram-famous.
3. **Jehwangsan Park (제황산공원)** — Cable car available (fee required). Jinhae Tower observation area.
4. **Naval Academy** — Public tours during festival only.

### B) Yeouido Spring Flower Festival (여의도 봄꽃축제)
- **Dates:** Approximately April 4-9, 2026 (exact dates TBA, check closer to date)
- **Peak Bloom:** April 7-12 in Seoul
- **Location:** Yeouido Hangang Park, Yunjung-ro Road
- **Admission:** FREE
- **Features:** 1,600-1,800 cherry trees creating a 1.8 km pink tunnel, food stalls, cultural performances, night illuminations
- **Access:** Subway Line 5 to Yeouinaru Station (Exit 1) or Line 9 to National Assembly Station (Exit 6). 5-minute walk.

### C) Gyeongju Cherry Blossom Festival (경주 벚꽃축제)
- **Dates:** Early April 2026 (typically first week of April)
- **Daereungwon Cherry Blossom Festival hours:** 1 PM – 10 PM
- **Light shows:** 8 PM and 9 PM
- **Admission:** FREE
- **Key Spots:** Bomun Lake (보문호, 9,000 trees over 8 km), Bulguksa Temple area
- **Gyeongju Sakura Marathon:** Typically early April
- **Access:** KTX to Singyeongju Station, then local bus

### D) Jeju Cherry Blossom Festival
- **Dates:** March 23 – April 1, 2026
- **Key Roads:** Jeonnong-ro (전농로, 1.2 km with night illumination), Noksan-ro (녹산로, rapeseed + cherry combo)
- **Unique:** King Cherry Trees (왕벚나무) — petals 1.5-2x larger and deeper pink than mainland varieties

### E) Daegu E-World Starlight Cherry Blossom Festival
- **Dates:** Late March – early April
- **Entry:** All-day ticket 50,000 KRW (discounted online ~28,600 KRW); Night ticket (after 5 PM) 35,700 KRW
- **Features:** Illuminated cherry blossoms at night, theme park rides

### F) Other Festivals
- **Hadong Hwagae Cherry Blossom Festival** — Sipri Cherry Blossom Road (화개십리벚꽃길), 5 km tunnel of 100+ year-old trees, early April
- **Yeosu Bloom and Run Festival** — Includes hwajeon-making activities

## 4. Best Cherry Blossom Spots — Detailed Guide

### SEOUL

#### Yeouido Hangang Park (여의도 한강공원)
- **Subway:** Yeouinaru Station (Line 5, Exit 1) or National Assembly Station (Line 9, Exit 6)
- **Walk:** 5 minutes
- **Entry:** Free
- **Best Time:** Weekday mornings 7-8 AM ("you'll practically have the tunnel to yourself")
- **Crowd Level:** EXTREMELY crowded weekends 11AM-4PM
- **Highlight:** 1,600+ trees, 1.8 km tunnel, Han River backdrop

#### Seokchon Lake (석촌호수)
- **Subway:** Jamsil Station (Line 2, Exit 3)
- **Walk:** 5 minutes
- **Entry:** Free
- **Best Time:** Evening for night illumination
- **Crowd Level:** Moderate to busy
- **Highlight:** Lake reflections with Lotte World Tower backdrop, 2.5 km path, paddleboat rentals

#### Gyeongbokgung Palace (경복궁)
- **Subway:** Gyeongbokgung Station (Line 3, Exit 5)
- **Walk:** 5 minutes
- **Entry:** 3,000 KRW (FREE if wearing hanbok)
- **Hanbok Rental:** 15,000-30,000 KRW for 2 hours
- **Best Time:** Morning
- **CLOSED ON TUESDAYS
- **Highlight:** Cherry blossoms with historical palace backdrop

#### Changgyeonggung Palace (창경궁)
- **Subway:** Hyehwa Station (Line 4, Exit 4)
- **Walk:** 10 minutes
- **Entry:** 1,000 KRW
- **Crowd Level:** Much less crowded than Gyeongbokgung
- **Highlight:** Weeping cherry blossoms reflected in Chundangji Pond

#### Seoul Forest (서울숲)
- **Subway:** Seoul Forest Station (Bundang Line, Exit 3)
- **Walk:** 5 minutes
- **Entry:** Free
- **Crowd Level:** About 1/3 of Yeouido's crowds
- **Highlight:** 1.2 million sqm eco-park with deer enclosures, cycling paths

#### Namsan Park / N Seoul Tower (남산공원)
- **Subway:** Myeong-dong Station (Line 4, Exit 3), 10-min walk to cable car
- **Entry:** Free (cable car and tower separate fees)
- **Best Time:** Blue hour (just after sunset) for tower + blossom photos
- **Highlight:** City skyline panorama framed by blossoms

#### Olympic Park (올림픽공원)
- **Subway:** Olympic Park Station (Line 5, Exit 3 or 4)
- **Entry:** Free
- **Crowd Level:** Spacious, relaxed setting
- **Highlight:** "One Tree Hill" — lone tree framed by cherry blossoms, very photogenic

#### Hidden Gems in Seoul:
- **Banpo Hummingway Road (반포 허밍웨이길)** — Quiet Han River path
- **Seoul National Cemetery (국립서울현충원)** — Weeping cherry trees, serene atmosphere
- **Seongnae-cheon Stream (성내천)** — Stream tunnel, surprisingly peaceful

### BUSAN

## 5. Practical Travel Tips

### Hotel Booking
- **Book 6 months in advance** (September for late March/April travel) to save 50%+ on rates
- Peak season prices surge significantly; last-minute bookings are expensive and limited
- **Seoul best areas:** Hongdae/Mapo (budget, near Yeouido), Jamsil/Gangnam (near Seokchon Lake), Jongno/Myeongdong (central, near palaces)
- **Busan best areas:** Haeundae, Seomyeon, Nampo-dong

### Hotel Price Ranges (per night, peak season):
- Budget hostels/guesthouses: $20-30
- 2-star hotels: from $24 (Hongdae, Mapo)
- 3-star hotels: from $37 (Myeongdong, Jongno)
- 4-star hotels: from $44 (Gangnam, Jamsil)
- Luxury: $150+

### Transportation
- **T-money Card:** Essential. Works on all subways, buses, and taxis. Purchase at any convenience store (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven). Rechargeable.
- **Seoul Metro:** Covers all major cherry blossom spots. Base fare ~1,250 KRW.
- **KTX (High-Speed Rail):** Seoul → Busan (2.5 hrs, ~59,800 KRW), Seoul → Gyeongju via Singyeongju (~2 hrs)
- **Intercity Buses:** Book via bustago.or.kr (buses to Jinhae sell out fast during festival)
- **Jinhae tip:** Take bus from Busan Seobu Terminal (Sasang Station). Much shorter than from Seoul.

### Weather (Late March – Early April)
- **Late March:** 2-10°C (36-50°F). Cold mornings, mild afternoons. Occasional rain.
- **Early April:** 8-17°C (46-63°F). Warmer but still chilly evenings (~6°C/43°F).
- **Daily temperature swing:** Can vary 10-15°C between morning and afternoon
- **Rain:** Frequent in spring. Carry umbrella always.

### What to Wear
- **Layered clothing is essential** — temperatures swing dramatically
- Light jacket or windbreaker (essential)
- Long-sleeved shirts, sweaters
- Jeans or comfortable pants
- Comfortable walking shoes (you'll walk 15,000-25,000 steps daily)
- Scarf for early mornings (especially late March)
- **Clear/transparent umbrella** — light rain creates "cherry blossom rain" (꽃비, kkotbi) with petals drifting through the air. A clear umbrella lets you see this while staying dry.

### What to Pack
- Portable charger (phone battery drains fast with photos)
- Picnic blanket (buy at Daiso for 3,000-5,000 KRW)
- Reusable water bottle
- Sunscreen (spring sun can be strong)
- Hat for sun protection

## 6. Photography Tips

### Timing
- **Golden hour (sunrise, ~6:30-7:30 AM):** Soft, warm light filtering through petals. Best for the "empty tunnel" look with no crowds.
- **Blue hour (just after sunset):** Perfect for blossoms with illuminated landmarks (N Seoul Tower, Lotte World Tower)
- **Overcast days:** Actually excellent — even, diffused light eliminates harsh shadows, makes pink petals glow

### Best Practices
- **Arrive by 7 AM** at popular spots for empty compositions
- **Shoot upward** through canopy for dramatic "ceiling of pink" shots
- **Use reflections:** Seokchon Lake, Bomun Lake, Chundangji Pond offer mirror-like surfaces
- **Include a focal point:** Person in hanbok, a bench, a bridge, temple roof
- **Portrait mode / shallow depth of field:** Isolate individual blossom branches against soft bokeh backgrounds
- **Night photography:** Use tripod for long exposures of illuminated blossoms. Yeouido, Seokchon Lake, and Jeonnong-ro (Jeju) all have night lighting.
- **"One Tree Hill" at Olympic Park:** Lone tree framed by cherry blossoms — extremely photogenic
- **Gyeonghwa Station:** Vintage train car with blossom tunnel — Instagram-famous
- **Rainy days:** Cherry blossom petals on wet surfaces create beautiful compositions. Fallen petals on streams = "petal rivers."

### Composition Ideas
- Cherry blossom tunnel (Yeouido, Jinhae Yeojwacheon)
- Blossoms framing traditional Korean architecture (palaces)
- Reflection shots on lake surfaces
- Petals falling in wind (burst mode)
- Night illumination with long exposure
- Contrast of pink blossoms against traditional Korean temple roofs

## 7. Seasonal Food & Treats

### Traditional Korean Spring Treats
- **Hwajeon (화전)** — Flower pancakes: Small pan-fried rice cakes made with glutinous rice flour, honey, and edible flower petals (cherry blossom, azalea). Pan-fried in sesame oil. Traditional spring picnic food (hwajeon nori = "flower cake play").
- **Cherry Blossom Rice Cakes** — Pink-tinted tteok with subtle floral flavor
- **Steamed Blossom Buns** — Sweet buns with cherry blossom flavoring

### Festival & Street Food
- **Cherry blossom bread** (Jinhae specialty)
- **Blossom-flavored ice cream** (available at Jinhae and major festival sites)
- **Sakura soda/sparkling water** — Lightly floral, often pink-tinted
- Regular Korean street food at festivals: tteok-bokki, hotteok (sweet pancakes), odeng (fish cake skewers), bungeoppang (fish-shaped pastries)

### Convenience Store & Cafe Seasonal Items
- Cherry blossom latte (Starbucks Korea and local cafes release seasonal versions)
- Sakura-flavored KitKats, Pocky (limited edition)
- Cherry blossom soju (limited releases from major brands)

## 10. Crowd Avoidance Strategy

| Time | Crowd Level | Rating |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Weekday 7-9 AM | Empty/minimal | BEST |
| Weekday 10 AM-12 PM | Moderate | Good |
| Weekday afternoon | Busy | OK |
| Weekend 11 AM-4 PM | Extremely crowded | AVOID |

**Pro Tips:**
- Arrive Tuesday or Wednesday, stay through Friday
- Visit less-known spots: Changgyeonggung over Gyeongbokgung, Seoul Forest over Yeouido
- For Jinhae: weekday visit + go beyond the crowded front sections of Yeojwacheon Stream
- Slightly before or after peak bloom = cheaper accommodations + thinner crowds (still beautiful)

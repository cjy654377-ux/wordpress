Arirang Album — 14 Tracks That Tell Korea's Story

The album title Arirang is no accident. The traditional Korean folk song has been sung for centuries as a symbol of longing, separation, and reunion — themes that resonate deeply with BTS's seven-year journey through military service, solo growth, and an unprecedented global fanbase that never stopped waiting.

Released on March 20, 2026 via Big Hit Music, Arirang is BTS's fifth studio album. With 14 tracks, it is their most expansive studio project to date. The album draws from Korean musical heritage while layering in the hip-hop, pop, and experimental sounds the group has always championed — a sonic homecoming as much as a personal one.

The album is available on all major platforms from release day:

  • Spotify
  • Apple Music
  • YouTube Music
  • Physical CD via Weverse Shop and global retailers
The name Arirang references Korea's most beloved folk song, designated as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage. By naming their comeback album after it, BTS signals a return to roots — both personal and national.

Early listener previews describe a tracklist that moves from introspective ballads in the opening acts to euphoric anthems by the final stretch. Expect solo showcases woven into the album's structure, alongside full-group tracks built for stadium sing-alongs. Every member brings the craft they developed during their solo runs — and it shows.
